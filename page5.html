Sharks are found in all seas.
They generally do not live in fresh water,
with a few exceptions such as the bull shark
and the river shark which can swim
both in seawater and freshwater.
Sharks are common down to
depths of 2,000 metres (7,000 ft),
and some live even deeper,
\ but they are almost entirely absent
below 3,000 metres (10,000 ft).
The deepest confirmed report of a shark is a
Portuguese dogfish at 3,700 metres (12,100 ft).
